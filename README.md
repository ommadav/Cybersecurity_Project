# National Cybersecurity Law for VIT's

A comprehensive cybersecurity law and policy framework designed for the fictional nation **VIT's**. This project establishes a complete legal, regulatory, and operational framework to address modern cyber threats, protect citizens' digital rights, secure critical infrastructure, and promote a safe digital ecosystem.

## Project Objectives

- Protect citizens' privacy and personal data
- Define cyber offenses and corresponding penalties
- Establish a national data protection framework
- Strengthen corporate and government cybersecurity responsibilities
- Create a National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA)
- Develop cyber incident reporting and response procedures
- Regulate AI, deepfakes, IoT, cloud computing, and digital identity systems
- Protect critical infrastructure sectors
- Promote cybersecurity awareness and education
- Enhance international cybersecurity cooperation

## Key Features

- Citizens' Digital Rights Charter
- Cybercrime Classification & Punishment Framework
- Data Protection and Privacy Regulations
- Corporate Compliance Requirements
- Government Cybersecurity Responsibilities
- Cyber Incident Reporting Framework
- National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA)
- AI & Emerging Technology Regulations
- Critical Infrastructure Protection Policies
- International Cyber Cooperation Framework
- Real-World Inspired Cybercrime Case Studies
- Future Cybersecurity Recommendations (2025–2035)

## Technologies & Standards Referenced

- ISO/IEC 27001
- CERT-In (India)
- NCIIPC (India)
- Singapore Cybersecurity Act
- EU AI Act
- Budapest Convention on Cybercrime
- INTERPOL Cybercrime Framework

## Outcome

This project demonstrates how a modern nation can establish a robust cybersecurity legal framework that balances security, privacy, innovation, and digital governance while addressing emerging cyber threats and technological advancements.
